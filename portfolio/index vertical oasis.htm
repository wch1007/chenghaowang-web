中关村垂直绿洲——未来城市公共空间更新 (Zhongguancun Vertical Oasis: Future Urban Public Space Renewal)

This project addresses the decline of Zhongguancun Street's core area—once a thriving innovation hub—following pandemic impacts and flawed urban design. Field investigations identified critical issues: oppressive scale imbalance between oversized buildings and chaotic residual spaces; sterile functionality that reduced the area to mere thoroughfares lacking停留价值; monotonous vegetation with limited species diversity; and prohibitive elevation changes with poorly designed steps and sunken plazas that discouraged pedestrian engagement. Our response integrates futuristic movable architecture with botanical and landscape expertise, creating a "vertical oasis" that compresses alpine ecosystems into urban fabric. This innovative approach transforms 50 meters of elevation difference into a vibrant, multi-functional public realm that reconnects people with nature while revitalizing commercial activity.
本项目针对曾是繁华创新中心的中关村大街核心区在疫情影响与设计缺陷下的衰退问题。实地调研发现关键问题：过大建筑与杂乱剩余空间形成压抑的尺度失衡；单调功能使区域沦为缺乏停留价值的纯粹通道；植被种类单一；高差设计不合理，台阶与下沉广场设置不当，阻碍行人参与。 我们的解决方案将未来主义可动建筑与植物学、景观学专业知识相结合，创造出将高山生态系统压缩到城市肌理中的"垂直绿洲"。这种创新方法将50米高差转化为充满活力的多功能公共领域，在重振商业活动的同时，重建人与自然的连接。

高山垂直带的城市压缩技术 (Urban Compression of Alpine Vertical Zones)

The design's ecological foundation lies in the research of Gongga Mountain's vertical natural zones—from subtropical valleys to alpine tundra. Through ecological simulation software, we identified 12 representative plant communities and successfully compressed their climatic requirements into the 50-meter elevation range of Zhongguancun Square. Technical innovations include microclimate control systems: geothermal heating for "subalpine" zones, misting systems simulating monsoon conditions, and adjustable light filters replicating different altitudinal solar intensities. This allows species from vastly different climates—rhododendrons from temperate forests, junipers from montane zones, and alpine meadow grasses—to coexist within a single urban space, creating a living botanical museum that educates while refreshing.
设计的生态基础源于对贡嘎山垂直自然带的研究——从亚热带河谷到高山苔原。通过生态模拟软件，我们筛选出12个代表性植物群落，并成功将其气候需求压缩到中关村广场50米的高程范围内。 技术创新包括微气候控制系统："亚高山"区域的地热供暖、模拟季风条件的喷雾系统，以及复制不同海拔太阳强度的可调光过滤器。这使得来自截然不同气候带的物种——温带森林的杜鹃花、山地的杜松和高山草甸草——能够在单一城市空间中共存，创造出兼具教育与休憩功能的活植物博物馆。

可动建筑与多功能空间网络 (Movable Architecture & Multi-functional Space Network)

Central to the design is a network of adaptable architectural elements that respond to changing needs: modular pavilions with hydraulic systems that adjust their form for markets, exhibitions, or performances; retractable canopies that expand to create covered plazas during rain or extreme heat; and movable planters that reconfigure to alter circulation patterns. These elements integrate with programmatic spaces distributed across vertical levels: a sunken commercial street with natural light wells; a sports plaza with convertible courts; an urban cable car offering elevated views of the entire complex; and innovative recreational facilities including a sky pool and indoor ski slope—all connected through barrier-free vertical circulation systems that transform the problematic elevation changes into experiential opportunities.
设计核心是适应需求变化的可动建筑元素网络：带液压系统的模块化亭阁可调整形态以适应市集、展览或表演；可伸缩顶棚在雨天或酷暑时展开形成有顶广场；可移动种植池通过重组改变流线模式。 这些元素与分布在垂直层面的功能空间整合：带自然光井的下沉商业街；带可转换球场的运动广场；提供综合体全景视角的城市缆车；以及创新休闲设施（包括空中泳池与室内滑雪场）——全部通过无障碍垂直交通系统连接，将原本的高差问题转化为体验亮点。

垂直空间的立体串联 (Three-dimensional Connection of Vertical Spaces)

Breaking free from traditional planar urban thinking, the design creates a fully integrated vertical ecosystem through strategic spatial sequencing. A continuous circulation system—combining ramps, scenic elevators, and spiral pathways—connects underground levels with ground plazas and elevated gardens, turning vertical movement into an engaging journey through diverse microclimates and experiences. GIS analysis informed the optimal distribution of functions across elevations: active commercial spaces at street level, quieter recreational areas in mid-levels, and panoramic observation points at higher elevations. This vertical zoning respects natural light conditions while creating a diverse programmatic offer that encourages prolonged停留 and exploration rather than mere passage.
设计突破传统平面城市思维，通过策略性空间序列创造完全整合的垂直生态系统。连续的交通系统——结合坡道、景观电梯与螺旋路径——连接地下层与地面广场及高空花园，将垂直移动转化为穿越多样微气候与体验的趣味旅程。 GIS分析指导了各高程功能的优化分布：街道层的活跃商业空间、中层的安静休闲区、高层的全景观景点。这种垂直分区尊重自然光照条件，同时创造多样化功能供给，鼓励长时间停留与探索而非单纯通行。

总结 (Conclusion)

This project reimagines urban public space as a dynamic, vertically integrated ecosystem that addresses both functional deficits and environmental disconnection. By compressing natural landscapes into urban form and embracing movable architecture, it transforms a declining commercial district into a resilient, adaptable public realm that responds to both human needs and ecological principles. The design demonstrates how innovative thinking can turn urban challenges—scale imbalance, elevation issues, monotony—into opportunities for creating more engaging, nature-connected cities. It stands as a model for post-pandemic urban renewal that prioritizes human experience, biodiversity, and functional flexibility.
本项目将城市公共空间重新想象为动态、垂直整合的生态系统，同时解决功能缺陷与环境疏离问题。通过将自然景观压缩为城市形态并采用可动建筑，将衰退的商业区转变为能响应人类需求与生态原则的弹性适应公共领域。 设计展示了创新思维如何将城市挑战——尺度失衡、高差问题、单调乏味——转化为创造更具吸引力、更贴近自然的城市的机遇。它成为后疫情时代城市更新的典范，优先考虑人类体验、生物多样性与功能灵活性。
