长辛店大街——老街新生博物馆建筑群 (Changxindian Street: Museum Complex for a Reborn Historic District)

This project emerged from the urgency to save Changxindian Street—where 400 years of commercial history, revolutionary heritage, and grassroots life converge—from imminent demolition. As a key node on the ancient post road from Beijing to Zhili, the street witnessed the prosperity of明清commerce, the roar of early 20th-century railway workshops, and the heroic struggles of railway workers. Field investigations revealed layers of collective memory embedded in its fabric: the 17th-century Huoshen Temple, the Qing-dynasty Mosque, weathered shopfronts with carved lintels, and oral histories of generations of residents. This project weaves these fragments into a cohesive museum complex, preserving the street's soul while breathing new life into its aging form.
本项目源于拯救长辛店大街的迫切需求——这条汇聚400年商业史、革命遗产与市井生活的老街正面临拆除危机。作为京畿古道上的关键节点，这里见证了明清商帮的繁华、20世纪初铁路工厂的轰鸣，以及铁路工人的英勇抗争。实地调研揭示了其肌理中嵌入的多重集体记忆：17世纪的火神庙、清代清真寺、带雕花门楣的风化铺面，以及代代居民的口述史。本项目将这些片段编织成连贯的博物馆建筑群，在保留街道灵魂的同时，为其衰老的形态注入新生。

记忆锚点的识别与活化 (Identification & Activation of Memory Anchors)

Over 3 months of fieldwork, the project documented 28 historically significant structures and conducted 87 interviews with local residents aged 60+. Through thematic mapping, it identified 12 "memory anchors"—spaces repeatedly mentioned as承载 collective identity: Huoshen Temple's glazed roof, the Mosque's arched gate, the old post office's wooden counters, and the "Iron Man" sculpture from the February 7th Movement. Collaboration with the curator of Changxindian Erqi Factory Museum provided critical historical context, ensuring accurate preservation of industrial relics and revolutionary sites. These anchors became the nuclei of the museum complex, with new construction designed to frame and celebrate them rather than overshadow their presence.
经过3个月实地工作，项目记录了28处历史建筑，并对87位60岁以上当地居民进行访谈。通过主题Mapping，识别出12个"记忆锚点"——被反复提及的集体身份载体：火神庙的琉璃瓦顶、清真寺的拱形山门、老邮局的木质柜台，以及二七运动"铁人"雕塑。 与长辛店二七厂博物馆馆长的合作提供了关键历史背景，确保工业遗迹与革命旧址的准确保护。这些锚点成为博物馆建筑群的核心，新建部分旨在衬托和彰显它们，而非掩盖其存在。

线性空间线索与多元建筑语言 (Linear Spatial Thread & Diverse Architectural Language)

The design hinges on a central spatial thread—a 600-meter pedestrian path that connects all memory anchors, echoing the street's original commercial axis. This thread is articulated through diverse architectural expressions: a covered corridor bridging across a narrow alley, preserving the intimacy of traditional hutong; a stone archway marking the transition from civilian to religious zones (connecting Huoshen Temple and Mosque); a 15-meter observation tower offering panoramic views of the street's historic skyline; and a water channel reinterpreting the old drainage system, now reflecting the facades of preserved shopfronts. Each architectural element responds to its context: the corridor's timber structure references local folk construction, while the tower's brickwork echoes the industrial chimneys of nearby Erqi Factory. This diversity of form ensures the new intervention speaks both to the street's layered history and its contemporary renewal.
设计以一条核心空间线索为枢纽——600米步行路径串联所有记忆锚点，呼应街道原有的商业轴线。这条线索通过多元建筑语言呈现：过街廊桥跨越窄巷，保留传统胡同的亲密尺度；石拱门标志着从市井到宗教区的过渡（连接火神庙与清真寺）；15米高的观景塔提供街道历史天际线的全景视角；水渠重新诠释旧排水系统，如今倒映着保留铺面的立面。 每个建筑元素都回应其语境：廊桥的木结构参考当地民间建造，塔楼的砖石工艺呼应附近二七厂的工业烟囱。这种形式多样性确保新介入既能对话街道的层积历史，又能体现其当代更新。

功能适配与尺度呼应 (Functional Adaptation & Scale Response)

The museum complex comprises 5 specialized volumes, each precisely scaled to fit the street's original肌理: a 300㎡ exhibition hall for railway history (housed in a renovated 1950s workshop), a 150㎡ performance space for local operas (with acoustic design adapted to traditional storytelling), a 100㎡ reception center (incorporating reclaimed doorframes from demolished houses), a 80㎡ oral history archive, and a 200㎡ community square with movable seating. All new construction adheres to the street's 3-5m width rhythm and 6-8m height limit, ensuring the museum complex feels like a natural extension rather than an intrusion. Materials—reclaimed brick, local cypress, and weathered steel—further blur the boundary between old and new, creating a seamless narrative of continuity.
博物馆建筑群包含5个专项体量，每个都精准适配街道原有肌理：300㎡铁路历史展厅（改造自1950年代车间）、150㎡地方戏曲演艺空间（声学设计适配传统说书形式）、100㎡接待中心（融入拆迁房屋回收的门框）、80㎡口述史档案馆，以及200㎡带可移动座椅的社区广场。 所有新建部分遵循街道3-5米的宽度节奏与6-8米的高度限制，确保博物馆建筑群成为自然延伸而非外来介入。材料——再生砖、本地柏木与耐候钢——进一步模糊新旧边界，创造连贯的叙事性。

总结 (Conclusion)

This project reimagines historic preservation not as frozen nostalgia, but as a living dialogue between past and present. By centering on collective memory rather than mere architectural relics, it transforms Changxindian Street into a museum that is itself part of the community—where residents don't just visit history, but live within it. The design proves that urban renewal can honor the past while accommodating the future, turning the threat of demolition into an opportunity to reaffirm cultural identity.
本项目将历史保护重新想象为过去与现在的鲜活对话，而非僵化的怀旧。通过聚焦集体记忆而非单纯建筑遗迹，将长辛店大街转变为社区自身的一部分——居民不仅"参观"历史，更生活于其中。设计证明城市更新可以尊重过去同时包容未来，将拆除威胁转化为重申文化身份的机遇。
